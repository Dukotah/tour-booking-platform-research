## FareHarbor — Deep Dive

### What They Do Well
- **Market dominance:** Largest tour booking platform in the US. Massive brand recognition — operators often discover it first.
- **Feature breadth:** Scheduling, crew assignment, resource management (prevents double-booking shared assets), channel manager (connects OTAs), reseller network, affiliate bookings.
- **Reporting depth:** Claims 10M+ report combinations — powerful filtering by date, activity, staff, channel, and more.
- **Integrations:** Connects to Viator, Expedia, GetYourGuide, Google Things To Do, and more. Google Reserve integration drives bookings directly from Google Search/Maps.
- **24/7 support:** Widely praised for knowledgeable, responsive support — a genuine differentiator.
- **Custom manifests:** Operators can save filtered manifest views and assign them to specific staff roles.
- **No monthly fee:** "Free" to get started — only charges when bookings happen.

### What They Do Poorly
- **Fees are brutal and opaque:** 6% on all online/dashboard bookings, 2% on API bookings. Fee is added to the customer price by default and not prominently disclosed at checkout — customers see a surprise fee, operators look deceptive. FareHarbor has also been reported to round up all transactions and pocket the "change" on top of the 6%.
- **Waivers are not native:** FareHarbor integrates with Smartwaiver (a separate product) — waivers are NOT built in. Operators pay separately for Smartwaiver.
- **Checkout is slow:** One operator reported a "14-second spinning wheel of death" during checkout — a direct conversion killer. No Apple Pay or Google Pay natively.
- **Complex dashboard:** Steep learning curve, described as overwhelming for small/simple operators. Not intuitive for new staff to learn quickly.
- **No customer self-service portal:** Customers cannot log in, view their bookings, reschedule, or cancel online. All changes go through staff.
- **No dynamic pricing:** Prices are static. No demand-based, early-bird, or group-tier pricing automation.
- **No product variants:** Merchandise items can't have size/color options.
- **Limited POS for walk-ups:** While present, operators report manual credit card entry and limited payment flexibility.
- **Website builder is expensive:** $5,000/year or $499/month for their website building service.
- **Tiered pricing is difficult to implement:** One operator reported a 20-day back-and-forth with support trying to set up tiered pricing with no resolution — losing revenue the entire time.
- **No accounting integrations:** No QuickBooks or Xero sync. Manual data export required.
- **No public API or webhooks:** Closed ecosystem.
- **6% fee on manual bookings:** Even when staff enters a walk-up booking in the dashboard, the 6% fee applies.

### Why Operators Leave FareHarbor
1. The 6% fee is unsustainable as volume grows — one operator reported $20K less revenue in a single month after their fees were fully applied
2. Customers complain about the surprise fee at checkout, damaging the operator's brand
3. Feature requests go unanswered for months
4. Dashboard is too complex for seasonal staff who need to be trained quickly
5. No way to absorb or waive the booking fee — it's forced onto the customer
6. Owned by Booking.com — operators feel they're feeding a competitor's ecosystem

## Singenuity — Deep Dive

### What They Do Well
- **Built by operators:** Team is made up of former and current tour operators — the product reflects real operational knowledge
- **Feature set is strong:** Online + POS in one place, OTA connections, affiliate network, digital waivers (native, not third-party), self-serve kiosks, photo capture for revenue, transportation management, packages, priority booking (automatically fills specific time slots first for capacity optimization)
- **Priority booking feature:** Uniquely smart — automatically funnels customers to fill specific slots first, reducing half-empty departures
- **Simple pricing:** 4% flat on all reservations — no surprises
- **Responsive support:** Positive reviews on support quality
- **Positive reviews overall:** Fewer reviews than FareHarbor but high satisfaction scores

### What They Do Poorly
- **Still charges 4% per booking:** 4% is more transparent than FareHarbor's 6% but still significant at scale — $40 on every $1,000 in bookings
- **Smaller platform, smaller network:** Less OTA reach, smaller reseller marketplace than FareHarbor
- **Limited review volume:** Fewer public reviews makes it harder for operators to evaluate confidently before switching
- **No customer self-service portal:** Same gap as FareHarbor
- **No dynamic pricing**
- **No public API**
- **No accounting integrations**
- **Limited custom reporting**

## Peek Pro — Deep Dive

### What They Do Well
- **Marketing automation:** Abandoned cart recovery, customer retargeting, Peek Capital (business loans), dynamic pricing (XolaBot-style)
- **Pre-tipping at checkout:** One of few platforms that prompts for tip during online checkout — operators report meaningful tip revenue
- **Integrated waivers:** Waivers built in
- **Upsell tools:** Add-on and bundle upsell at checkout
- **Reseller network:** Peer marketplace for cross-operator distribution

### What They Do Poorly
- **Fees are opaque and high:** Won't publish pricing — you must contact sales. Reviews suggest up to 6% + setup fees. Fees described as "fluctuating" and "confusing" — operators can't predict costs
- **Customer support collapse:** Multiple reviews describe completely unresponsive support — no returned calls, no email replies — for what operators describe as urgent operational issues. "Customer service doesn't exist."
- **Payment fees are separate from booking fees:** Operators are surprised to find merchant processing fees are additional
- **Not suitable for simple operations:** Feature-heavy platform that's overkill for a single-activity operator

## Xola — Deep Dive

### What They Do Well
- **Conversion focus:** XolaBot for dynamic pricing, surge pricing, abandoned cart recovery, and creating new time slots during high-demand periods
- **Analytics:** Strong analytics and conversion tracking — operators can see where in the funnel they're losing bookings
- **Flexibility:** Highly customizable scheduling, pricing rules, and customer flows
- **Support:** Among the highest-rated support in the category
- **Flat(ish) pricing:** 1.9% + $0.30 per booking is lower than FareHarbor/Peek Pro

### What They Do Poorly
- **No POS integration:** A significant gap — operators with walk-up customers have no native in-person POS
- **No gratuity at POS:** Pre-gratuity works online but limited at in-person sale
- **Expensive for small operators**
- **No free trial**
- **Split payment editing is difficult**
- **Bulk guest entry is limited**

## Bókun — Deep Dive

- **Low fees:** 1–1.5% booking fee, 0% on Viator and offline bookings
- **Free plan available:** Basic features at no cost — low barrier to entry
- **Channel distribution:** Strong connection to OTA ecosystem (Expedia, GetYourGuide, Viator)

### What They Do Poorly

- **Not great for in-person/POS:** Built primarily for online distribution, not walk-up operations

## Bookeo — The Hidden Gem

- **Predictable cost:** Operators know exactly what they pay every month regardless of how many bookings come in
- **All core features included:** Scheduling, online booking, email confirmations, customer management

### What They Do Poorly
- **Dated UI:** One of the older platforms — the interface shows its age
- **No POS / Register app**
- **Limited waivers**
- **No real-time updates**
- **No channel manager or reseller network**
- **Limited reporting**

## Marina / Boat-Specific Platforms

### SpeedyDock
- Purpose-built for boat rental, boat clubs, and dry-stack marinas
- Features: Online booking, fleet management, automated dispatching, digital waivers, maintenance tracking, CRM, launch scheduling, slip assignments
- Real-time notifications to customers when their boat is ready (launch complete)
- Customers can check/modify boat reservation or launch from any device
- Staff "leaderboard" feature (gamifies launch speed for dock crews)
- **Gap:** No dynamic pricing, no full POS for retail/merchandise, limited reporting, no public API

### BoatCloud
- Features: Online bookings, fleet inventory management, automated payment processing, real-time availability syncing, dynamic pricing, CRM, multi-channel availability sync
- Integrates with DockMaster for customer and boat data sync
- Pricing: $49/month (Starter) with 1.9% + $1.50/booking fee
- **Gap:** Per-booking fee model gets expensive at scale, limited POS, no waivers

### DockMaster
- Full marina management: slips, dry storage, fuel dock, service/work orders, billing, occupancy tracking
- $165+/month
- Reviews: Powerful but UI is "extremely outdated", steep learning curve, requires understanding unique naming conventions just to generate basic reports
- **This is not a booking platform** — it's marina operations software. Operators often use DockMaster + a separate booking tool.

### RentalTide
- AI-powered dynamic pricing + GPS fleet tracking + digital waivers + marina operations
- Zero monthly fee (per-transaction model)
- Newest entrant — limited reviews

## The Universal Gaps Across ALL Platforms

These features are **missing or weak on every platform** — building all of them well is the clearest path to operator switching:

### 1. Customer Self-Service Portal
**Missing on:** FareHarbor, Singenuity, Peek Pro, Bookeo, SpeedyDock, BoatCloud, DockMaster

Customers cannot manage their own bookings. Every reschedule request, cancellation, or question goes to staff. This generates enormous front-desk workload and is the #1 complaint from operators' own customers.

**The win:** Token-based booking management (no login required — magic link in confirmation email). View booking, reschedule within policy, cancel, download receipt, view waiver status.

### 2. Native Dynamic / Flexible Pricing
**Missing on:** FareHarbor, Singenuity, Peek Pro, Bookeo, SpeedyDock, BoatCloud, DockMaster
**Weak on:** Xola (surge only), Bókun (limited)

No platform does demand-based pricing, early-bird pricing, last-minute discounts, AND group volume tiers all in one configurable system.

**The win:** Rules engine: "If availability drops below 20%, raise price 15%." "Book 14+ days ahead, save $10." "Group of 8+, $5 off per person." Visual price calendar on the booking site.

### 3. True Offline Mode for Register/POS
**Missing on:** All platforms — FareHarbor has no offline mode, Singenuity has none, SpeedyDock has limited
**Weak on:** Some have basic caching but no reliable offline queue

Outdoor operators — marinas, ziplines, farms — routinely have spotty connectivity. No platform has built a reliable offline-first Register app.

**The win:** Service Worker + IndexedDB queue. Manifest loads from cache. Check-ins queue locally. Visual sync indicator. Merges when reconnected.

### 4. Accounting Integrations
**Missing on:** Every platform listed above

No tour/activity booking platform has native QuickBooks or Xero sync. Operators spend hours per week manually exporting and importing. Accountants list this as a top frustration.

**The win:** Native QuickBooks Online sync (invoices, payments, refunds, tax). Chart of accounts mapping UI. Daily reconciliation export. Sync log.

### 5. Public API + Webhooks
**Missing on:** Every platform listed above

None of the major platforms offer a public REST API or webhook system. Operators cannot connect their CRM, accounting software, or custom tools. This is a major pain point for tech-savvy operators and a blocker for channel distribution.

**The win:** REST API with CRUD for bookings, customers, orders. API key management per operator. Webhook subscriptions for all major events. Zapier integration.

### 6. Modern Checkout UX (Apple Pay, One-Page, No Spinner)
**Missing on:** FareHarbor (slow, no Apple Pay), Singenuity, Bookeo, DockMaster, SpeedyDock
**Present on:** Bókun (Apple Pay), Peek Pro (limited)

Research shows: 60–70% of tour bookings are abandoned at checkout. Apple Pay integration alone increases conversion by 22%. A 14-second spinner (FareHarbor's documented problem) costs operators real money every day.

**The win:** Single-page booking flow (no reloads). Apple Pay + Google Pay at the top of the payment step. Skeleton loading states. Progress indicator. Sub-2-second responsiveness throughout.

### 7. Product Variants for Merchandise
**Missing on:** Every platform

None support size/color/style variants for merchandise. Operators selling branded apparel create a separate product per size — a major operational burden.

**The win:** Variant system with per-variant SKU, price, and stock. POS dropdown or button grid. Inventory tracked per variant.

### 8. Advanced Waiver Customization
**FareHarbor:** Not native (Smartwaiver integration = extra cost)
**Singenuity:** Native but basic
**Others:** Basic or missing

No platform supports conditional logic in waivers ("if age < 18, show guardian section"), waiver versioning with re-sign triggers, or annual expiry reminders.

**The win:** Native waiver builder with conditional logic, field types (signature, checkbox, date, dropdown), versioning, annual expiry, group/minor handling.

### 9. Custom Report Builder
**Missing on:** Every platform

All platforms offer fixed-template reports. No platform lets operators build custom reports, save them, or schedule email delivery.

**The win:** Report builder (select metrics, dimensions, date range, filters). Save and name. Schedule daily/weekly/monthly email delivery. PDF export with operator branding.

### 10. Transparent Flat Pricing (No Per-Booking Fee)
**Only platform with this:** Bookeo (but outdated product)

Every modern, well-featured platform charges 2–6% per booking. Operators with high volume pay enormous fees. There is no modern, full-featured platform with flat-rate SaaS pricing.

**The win:** Flat monthly subscription, zero per-booking fees. This is the single most powerful marketing message in the category. "Stop paying 6% to FareHarbor. Pay $X/month and keep every dollar."

## What Makes Operators Switch

Based on reviews across Capterra, G2, Trustpilot, and operator forums, here are the real switching triggers in priority order:

1. **Fees eating into margins** — The #1 reason. When operators calculate what they actually paid in booking fees over a year, the number is shocking.
2. **Customer service failures** — Specifically: not getting help during busy season when something breaks. One bad week of lost bookings is enough to trigger a switch.
3. **Customer complaints about fees** — When a customer asks "why is there a $18 booking fee on my $300 reservation?", operators lose trust in their platform.
4. **Missing features they specifically need** — Dynamic pricing, waivers, POS, offline mode — a critical missing feature will push a growing operator to look elsewhere.
5. **Slow/broken checkout** — Operators who monitor their booking funnel and see drop-offs at checkout start looking for alternatives.
6. **Platform ownership concerns** — FareHarbor being owned by Booking.com makes some operators nervous (feeding a competitor's ecosystem). Peek Pro/Bokun being backed by Viator/Tripadvisor raises similar concerns.

## Conversion Rate Intelligence

- **60–70% of tour bookings are abandoned at checkout** (industry average)
- **Apple Pay/Google Pay increases conversion 22–25%**
- **Stripe data:** 2x conversion rate when express checkout is shown early vs at the end of the form
- **Abandoned cart email sequences** (1hr + 24hr) recover 8–15% of abandoned bookings
- **Mobile-first checkout** (44px+ touch targets, no horizontal scroll, autofill) recovers 15–25% of lost mobile sales
- **Loading time:** Every 1-second delay in checkout increases abandonment by ~7%
- **Fee transparency:** Showing the total price including all fees upfront (before the final step) significantly reduces abandonment
